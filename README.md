## Executive Summary

Amazon India aimed to leverage insights from Amazon Brazil's e-commerce operations to better understand customer purchasing behavior, payment preferences, product performance, and seasonal sales trends. The dataset consisted of multiple relational tables representing customers, orders, products, sellers, payments, and order items, requiring end-to-end SQL analysis to uncover business opportunities. 

Analyze the relational database to answer real-world business questions, identify customer and sales patterns, evaluate product and payment performance, and generate actionable recommendations that could support strategic decision-making for Amazon India's growing marketplace. 

Imported and structured the dataset in PostgreSQL, performed data exploration and business analysis using SQL concepts including joins, aggregations, subqueries, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), window functions, ranking functions, CASE statements, and time-based analysis. Conducted customer segmentation, revenue trend analysis, seasonal analysis, payment behavior analysis, cumulative sales tracking, and month-over-month growth analysis to solve business-focused analytical problems.

The analysis identified customer purchasing patterns, high-value customer segments, dominant payment methods across different order value ranges, top-performing product categories, seasonal revenue trends, and monthly sales growth patterns. These insights demonstrated how relational database analytics can support pricing strategies, customer retention initiatives, targeted marketing campaigns, inventory planning, and data-driven business decision-making for a large-scale e-commerce platform.
