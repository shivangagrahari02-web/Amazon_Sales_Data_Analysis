## Executive Summary

Amazon India aimed to leverage Amazon Brazil's transactional data to understand customer purchasing behavior, payment preferences, product performance, and sales trends that could support strategic decisions for the Indian market. Using PostgreSQL, I analyzed a relational database consisting of six interconnected tables containing customer, order, product, payment, seller, and order item information. The project focused on solving real business problems through SQL while demonstrating analytical thinking and query optimization.

The analysis involved solving 19 business-driven SQL case studies using aggregations, joins, subqueries, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), window functions, ranking functions, and time-based analysis. Customer segmentation, seasonal sales, payment behavior, product pricing, revenue trends, cumulative sales, and month-over-month growth were analyzed to uncover meaningful business insights and convert raw transactional data into actionable recommendations.

The analysis revealed that **credit cards dominated low and medium-value transactions**, indicating the preferred payment method for most purchases. **A small set of product categories generated the highest revenue**, highlighting opportunities for targeted promotions and inventory optimization. Customer segmentation identified high-value and loyal customers suitable for personalized reward programs, while monthly and seasonal revenue analysis exposed clear demand fluctuations that can improve inventory planning and marketing campaigns. Additionally, cumulative sales and month-over-month growth analysis provided visibility into product lifecycle performance and changing payment trends, enabling more informed business decisions for revenue growth and customer retention.
